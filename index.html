art history

Art history offers a serious glance into the past by helping us look at some of the most revealing attributes of a time period. Not only are we able to gather feeling and emotions from a period of time, we can gather contextual clues to fascinations of the time, influential people of the time, as well as leading ideologies.

Here we have different comparisons on various aspects within the data for each of the four most influential time periods of art history: the Renaissance, the Post-Renaissance, the Contemporary period, and the Modern Art period. At the bottom is also each of the visualizations grouped by their respective styles for an overview of what art was like during that time period.

art subjects: presence of bodies or faces

An interesting starting place in analyzing art-history can begin with subject matters. The human form has consistently proven to be an important subject matter within history, as can be seen through the following four visualizations. We see as time moved forward into the modern and contemporary eras of art history, the common interest shifted as well. Looked at point-blank, we can see that the art world took a consistently decreasing jump away from focusing on the ‘face’ and ‘body’ towards the experimental and unfamiliar. Potentially our environments, scientific discoveries, and broad artistic explorations such as cubism, surfacing within the Modernist period of art. This can potentially be attributed to our growing human knowledge that continues to increase and blossom beyond that of the human form.

art emotions: emotions depicted over time

Diving deeper, we can look into the general emotion of a time period to help us contextualize a mood of an era. This helps us generate deeper questions about the events that lead to these emotions, as well as the categorical trends that came from it. We see the leading emotions such as ‘Trust’ and ‘Surprise’ in later movements butting heads, as their meanings contrast. Not only can we get a general sense of the contextual emotions of the time periods from the Renaissance to the Modern Art period, but we can investigate trends within the periods themselves to give us a deeper look into events that may have changed the course of art history, by correlating the spikes and their respective years.

art creators: ratings of individual artists

By analyzing the ratings of top artists of the times, not only are we able to get a glance at some of the most influential artists within each historical period, but we are able to take away some of the most influential artists of all time. Though we notice the renaissance holds a reasonable number of artists from which we can easily glance at and understand, the large increase in contributors to the artists pools within the Post-Renaissance and Modern Art period, work to demonstrate the open and expressive qualities of each movement to shift away from a popularized group of artists, and towards a larger creative evolution for the people.

art influences: pieces related to christianity

Possibly one of the most significant clues into the culture of a civilization involves the present ideologies. Christian iconography is one of the most symbolistically relevant themes within art history. Here, we investigate within each time period, where there are the largest number of references to Christianity, as well as the differences within each respective time period. We see a general downward trend of the presence of Christian references within titles as artistic time periods move forwards, as peoples scientific knowledge as well as general acceptance for unlikely opinions expanded.
